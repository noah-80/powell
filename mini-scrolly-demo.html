There’s a basilica that looks just like Powell Library and Royce Hall in Milan, Italy.
Meet Basilica of Sant'Ambrogio, one of the inspirations for the architecture for Powell Library, UCLA’s main undergraduate library.
Another inspiration is Basilica del Santo Sepolcro in Bologna, Italy,
which inspired Powell Library’s dome.
Powell Library’s architecture is full of historical references and intentional symbolism.
A Look Into Powell Library’s Symbolism and Architecture
Noah Hrung and Liam McGlynn
Powell’s main entrance has its historical counterpart, too.
The main entrance is a pediment, a temple-like entrance.
It is modeled after the entrance of the Basilica of San Zeno in Verona, Italy.
San Zeno’s entrance hosts a central Christ figure.
Powell’s entrance replaces Christ with an owl.
The owl is associated with Athena, Greek goddess of wisdom.
Above is a sun, with light representing knowledge. This idea of light is thematic of UCLA’s official motto: “Let there be light.”
Two worshippers kneel in respect to the owl.
